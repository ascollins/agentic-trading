# CMT Curriculum Reference — 2026 Program Guide
# All Three Levels: Foundational → Applied → Integrated

## LEVEL I — Foundational Principles of Technical Analysis

Core concepts, theories, terminology, tools and methods.
Format: 132 MCQ | 2 hours | 40-100 study hours

### Knowledge Domain Weights
- Theory and History: 38%
- Classical Techniques: 33%
- Advanced Techniques: 26%
- Ethics: 3%

### Section 1: Theory and History
- History of technical analysis, Dow Theory (primary/secondary/minor trends, confirmation, volume role)
- Markets, instruments, data handling (four asset classes, five tradeable instruments)
- Efficient Market Hypothesis (three forms, Joint Hypothesis problem, anomalies)
- Fibonacci sequence, Golden Ratio (0.618), retracements and extensions
- Behavioral finance (prospect theory, loss aversion, belief perseverance, information processing, emotional biases)

### Section 2: Charts — Market Price Data
- Chart construction: line, bar, candlestick (four basic price points)
- X-axis: data intervals, fractals, volume-based charts, point-and-figure (box size, reversal)
- Y-axis: arithmetic vs. logarithmic scales
- Volume and open interest display

### Section 3: Trend Analysis
- Trend definition and three primary movements (fractal nature)
- Wyckoff's four market phases (accumulation, markup, distribution, markdown)
- Retracements, pullbacks, throwbacks
- Trend identification: manual and mathematical (moving averages — single and dual)
- Trend-following process, reward-to-risk ratio, risk management principles
- Volume analysis (liquidity, participation, volume-price relationships)
- VWAP, open interest implications
- Market internals (breadth, leadership, volume measures)

### Section 4: Chart Pattern Analysis
- Classical patterns: reversal (H&S, double/triple tops/bottoms) and continuation (flags, pennants, triangles)
- Volume characteristics of patterns, gap types
- Candlestick construction, patterns (reversal, doji types), strengths/weaknesses
- Point-and-figure: construction, patterns, trendlines, price targets (horizontal/vertical counts)

### Section 5: Technical Indicators
- Moving averages: SMA, WMA, EMA, Wilder smoothing
- Indicator categories: momentum vs. volume
- Momentum indicators (RSI, MACD, stochastics), volume indicators
- Directional Movement Index (DMI): plots, interpretation, uses
- Bollinger Bands: calculation, first principles, basic application

### Section 6: Statistics for Technicians
- Descriptive statistics: mean, median, mode, geometric mean, standard deviation, variance
- Z-scores, outliers, scatterplots, Pearson's correlation, regression analysis
- Probability: law of large numbers, normal distribution, Empirical Rule, skew, kurtosis

### Section 7: Behavioral Finance
- Traditional economic assumptions challenged
- Prospect theory, loss aversion
- Belief perseverance biases, information processing biases, emotional biases

### Section 8: Sentiment
- Sentiment definition, crowd psychology, contrary opinion
- Market-data sentiment: VIX, futures OI, options volume/OI, COT report, short interest, insider activity
- External sentiment: news, advisories, mutual fund cash flows

### Section 9: Cycle Analysis
- Cycle foundations: two types, three defining characteristics
- Hurst's seven Principles of Commonality, harmonics, composite waves
- Left/right translation, dominant cycles
- Common economic cycles, seasonal cycles

### Section 10: Comparative Market Analysis
- Equities (sectors, capitalization, corporate actions)
- Indexes (weighting methods, survivorship bias)
- Fixed income (price/yield relationship, yield curve, credit spread)
- Futures (contract terms, OI, data challenges)
- ETPs (ETF vs. ETN, leveraged products)
- Foreign exchange (base/quote, majors/crosses)
- Digital assets (blockchain, on-chain data, TA applicability)
- Options (terms, Greeks, implied volatility)
- Relative strength (principles, absolute vs. relative trend, ranking methods)

### Section 11: Volatility Analysis
- Volatility definition, historical vs. implied
- True Range, Bollinger Bands, Keltner Channels
- Options-derived volatility, VIX (measurement, S&P 500 relationship, spikes)

### Section 12: Systems and Quantitative Methods
- Quantitative analysis and scientific method applied to investing
- Trigger rules, filter rules, value rules
- Signals, strategies, models
- Survivorship bias in testing
- Backtesting vs. signal testing

## LEVEL II — Application of Technical Analysis Methods

Applying concepts, tools, and methods to identify opportunities and manage risk.
Format: 170 MCQ | 4 hours | 100-140 study hours

### Knowledge Domain Weights
- Classical Techniques: 40%
- Advanced Techniques: 40%
- Application of TA: 10% (new domain)
- Theory and History: 7%
- Ethics: 3%

### Section 1: Theory and History
- Alpha vs. Beta, EMH vs. AMH vs. FMH
- Anomalous supply-demand scenarios
- Fusion analysis: technicians + fundamentalists working together

### Section 2: Behavioral Finance
- Market bubble anatomy (five stages)
- Chart-based assessment of bubble stages

### Section 3: Charts — Advanced
- Multiple data intervals for trend identification
- Intraday data sampling challenges
- Market Profile (basic principles)

### Section 4: Chart Pattern Analysis (Advanced)
- Price target calculation for reversal/continuation patterns
- Breakout confirmation: 3% rule vs. ATR multiple rule
- Rangebound vs. breakout tactics
- Candlestick patterns in practice (volume, support/resistance zones, confirmation)
- Incomplete/imperfect patterns, abandoned baby vs. island reversal
- Integration of candlesticks into broader chart analysis
- Point-and-figure: complex patterns, vertical/horizontal count projections

### Section 5: Trend Analysis (Advanced)
- Four phases of price-volume trends
- Volume interpretation in context of price phases
- Market internals: A/D line, up/down volume, breadth indicators
- Leadership indicators, qualitative internal analysis

### Section 6: Volatility Analysis (Advanced)
- VIX calculation of expected volatility and price ranges
- Volatility Risk Premium (VRP): definition, capturing via options
- VIX term structure: contango vs. backwardation
- VIX futures intermonth spreads as early warning signals
- VIX calls vs. S&P 500 puts for downside risk management

### Section 7: Sentiment (Advanced)
- Insider activity analysis (buying vs. selling)
- Short interest ratio
- Investor/professional surveys
- Derivatives sentiment: futures OI with price context, COT report analysis
- Put/call ratios, options market volatility data

### Section 8: Statistics (Advanced)
- Inferential statistics: hypothesis testing
- Steps, possible errors, framing statistical tests

### Section 9: Technical Indicators (Advanced)
- Momentum: velocity vs. acceleration
- Overbought/oversold in different market regimes
- Momentum divergences (types, significance, variables)
- Momentum reversals vs. price reversals
- Trend changes in momentum, buy/sell signals, valid signal criteria
- VWAP: original purpose, execution quality, buyer/seller control, intraday volume pattern
- Bollinger Bands advanced: %b, BandWidth, volatility cyclicality, Two-Bar Reversals, Squeezes/Bulges

### Section 10: Comparative Market Analysis (Advanced)
- Relative Rotation Graphs (RRG)
- JdK RS-Ratio and RS-Momentum as building blocks
- Ideal rotation sequence, benchmark role

### Section 11: Cycle Analysis (Advanced)
- Mid-cycle dip, 3/4-cycle high, inversions
- Rounded tops vs. V-bottoms (cyclical explanation)
- Left/right translation implications
- Centered moving average envelopes, valid trendlines
- Applied cycle analysis: finding vs. phasing cycles, spectrograms
- Harmonic phasing
- Seasonal analysis: detrending methods, annual cycle
- Elliott Wave: complete theory (motive/corrective waves, notation, extensions, diagonals)
- Corrective wave characteristics (alternation, depth)
- Fibonacci relationships in Elliott Wave
- Time-based tools combined with price-based tools

### Section 12: Systems and Quantitative Methods (Advanced)
- Five common trading models, go/no-go metrics
- Four signal generation methods, seven steps to profitable system
- Quantitative process steps
- Trigger/filter/value rules comparison
- Trade measures: annualised return, volatility, total return, CAGR, max drawdown, profit factor, expected value
- Performance ratios: Sharpe, Information, Sortino, Calmar
- Stop calculations, robustness in optimisation

## LEVEL III — The Work of a Technical Analyst

Integrating concepts and tools into analysing market action and managing positions/risk.
Format: MCQ + Short Answer | 4 hours | 120-160 study hours

### Knowledge Domain Weights
- Application of Technical Analysis: 95%
- Ethics: 5%

### Section 1: Quantifying and Managing Risk
- Utility of gambles: risk-seeking, risk-neutral, risk-averse investors
- Risk appetite diminishing with portfolio growth
- Volatility calculation across periods
- VaR and CVaR concepts
- Leverage effects on risk/reward
- Risk targeting methods
- Diversification types and common mistakes
- Uncorrelated security selection

### Section 2: Classical Chart Analysis (Integration)
- Trade design: price objectives, entries, stops (3% rule, ATR multiple)
- Reward-to-risk ratio assessment
- Failed pattern trading
- Candlesticks within broader TA framework (Western tools + candles)
- Confirmation methods, reliability assessment
- Candlestick integration into risk management
- Point-and-figure case studies: pattern testing, volatility gauging
- Top-down PnF process, stop identification, position sizing from PnF

### Section 3: Trend Analysis (Integration)
- Internal indicators in relation to price
- Using internals for market health assessment

### Section 4: Technical Indicators (Integration)
- Anchored VWAP (AVWAP): vs. conventional VWAP, interpretation principles
- Anchoring bias, time/price/event anchors
- Bollinger Bands advanced: reversal vs. continuation signals
- Multicollinearity in indicator selection
- M tops and W bottoms with %b

### Section 5: Volatility Analysis (Integration)
- VVIX (volatility of VIX), VIX vs. VVIX term structures
- SKEW Index (tail risk measurement)
- MOVE Index (bond market volatility)
- VIX as diversification tool vs. hedge
- Three strategy types harnessing implied volatility
- Three rules for improving success probability

### Section 6: Comparative Market Analysis (Integration)
- RRG advanced: open vs. closed universes
- RRGs in portfolio management
- Top-down stock selection via RRG
- RRG for FX markets

### Section 7: Systems and Quantitative Methods (Integration)
- Backtesting statistics: time-series features, log returns, statistical concerns
- Systematic trading: elements, model development, financial risk
- Mark-to-market, risk-per-trade vs. risk-per-unit-of-time
- Counter-trend trading (two approaches, basic model)
- Volatility-based position sizing
- Tactical asset allocation (All-Weather portfolio improvements)
- Expectancy formula, trading system comparison
- Advanced quantitative concepts

### Section 8: Portfolio Management Topics
- Fusion analysis: P = (F * V)S formula
- Stock selection combining fundamentals, trend, relative outperformance
- Long-term trend following vs. swing trading
- Relative strength and sector rotation strategies
- Tactical asset allocation, absolute vs. relative prices
- Top-down perspective on risk assessment
- Dynamic portfolio management
- **Digital assets in portfolios**: Bitcoin as benchmark, correlation with asset classes, portfolio allocation impact, macro drivers (currency debasement, economic cycles, USD Index)
- **On-chain metrics** as digital asset fundamentals
- Reflexivity in digital assets
- Sector analysis and traditional RS/breadth applied to digital assets
- Contrarianism, crowd behaviour, groupthink (identification, consequences, mitigation)
